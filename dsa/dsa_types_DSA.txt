# Types of Algorithms
 - Simple recursive Algorithms
 - Divide and Conquer algorithms
 - Dynamic programming algorithms
 - Greedy algorithms 
 - Brute force algorithms
 - Randomized algorithms

1. Simple Recursive Algorithms
In Simple Language: 
                 A function that solves a problem by calling a smaller version of itself over and over until it reaches a clear stopping point.
Real-Life Example: You open your College folder, inside it you open Semester 4, inside that you open Operating Systems, and you keep opening folders until you finally hit your Notes.txt file.

2. Divide and Conquer Algorithms
In Simple Language: You break a massive, overwhelming problem down into smaller, manageable pieces, solve those pieces individually, and then combine the results.

3. Dynamic Programming Algorithms
In Simple Language: Similar to divide and conquer, but it specifically remembers past results so it never wastes time calculating the exact same thing twice.


4. Greedy Algorithms
In Simple Language: It makes the absolute best, most optimal choice at this exact moment without worrying about or planning for the future consequences.
Real-Life Example: If you are told to pick 3 items from a grocery store for free, you immediately grab the 3 most expensive items on the shelf closest to you.

5.Brute force Algorithm
In Simple Language: The most basic, straightforward approach that tries every single possible option one by one until it finally finds the correct answer.
Real-Life Example: If you forget a 4-digit suitcase combination, you manually try 0001, 0002, 0003, and so on, until the latch finally clicks open.

6. Randomized Algorithms
In Simple Language: An algorithm that uses a bit of randomness or chance during its process to make decisions and find a solution faster.

Real-Life Example: Flipping a coin to decide who gets the last slice of pizza instead of spending an hour arguing over who deserves it more.
